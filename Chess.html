Chess

Chess is a strategic 2 player boardgame. In which the 2 players being black and white control there army of pieces. Both players main goal is to checkmate the opponent.

Instruction

Requirements

  • 2 players needed

Set Up

  • Your board is a 8x8 square with files and ranks
  • Files respresent in a x-axis, while ranks respresent in a y-axis
  • And to be sure that it's in the right direction check the side of the board as it must have a light square in the bottom right corner (If not try to turn it until its a light square)
  • How the pieces are setup look at the image example (Queens are in there own color square)

How to Play?

  • Your goal is to checkmate the opponent King
  • A checkmate is when a King does not have any ways to prevent its self from being captured
  • Seen in the example image the king doesn't have anyways to prevent itself from being captured
    (Red respresents capture areas)
  • But there is another outcome from the game that being a draw
  • A draw is when an opponent doesn't have any legal moves
  • For example the image shows that the black king doesn't have anywhere else to go
  • But it isn't in the queens capture radius

Pawn

  • The pawn can only move 1 square
  • The pawn can also move 2 squares only on its first move (Beware En Passant)
  • The Pawn can attack foward diagonally in one square
  • Once it makes it to the end it can become any piece (except the King)

Bishop

  • Can move and attack diagonally in a infinite range

Rook

  • Can move and attack horizontally and vertically in a infinite range

Knight

  • Can move and attack in a L position
  • But only at the end of the L seen in the example image
  • It's the only piece that can jump over other pieces

Queen

  • The queen
    is the most powerful
    piece in your board
  • Can move and attack
    diagonally;horizontally
    ;vertically
    in a infinite range

King

  • The king is your most valuable piece
  • As losing it can cost you your entire game
  • Can move and attack
    diagonally;horizontally
    ;vertically
    but its range can only extend to one square

Check

  • If the King is being attacked move your King away from danger at all cost
  • Or you can block the sight of the opponent piece (Block?)

Capture

  • In order to capture a piece the piece must be in the line of sight
  • When it is move your piece toward that square that the piece you captured once stood

Block/Protect

  • Other pieces can block a piece from attacking a certain area
  • But this can create protection if its your piece that blocking
  • When looking at this image, if a piece were to capture the Knight the Rook can come in and take its place

Castle

  • A castle is when the King moves two sqaure to the right or left and the Rook is also infront of the King (All of this is in one move as seen in the gif)
  • Times you can't castle though:
    If the Rook you want to castle with already moved;
    If the King moved;
    If the area of the castle is being attacked

en passant

  • Is when the pawn captures a pawn that moved 2 squares ahead
  • You can only do this move if the opponent moved its pawn just now and after that you can't do it in any later turns
